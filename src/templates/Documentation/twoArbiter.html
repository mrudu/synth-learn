In this example, we consider three processes P and Q that perform operations on/interacts with a critical section. Our goal is to synthesize an efficient arbiter which will control access of these processes to the critical section.

Representation of Environment/System interaction in atomic propoisitons

The environment here represents the two processes. The following are the atomic propositions of the environment:

  1. Request Access: r(i)

    Request made by process i to enter the critical section

The system here is the arbiter and the atomic propositions of the outputs are as follows:
  1. Grant Access: g(i)

    Grant access to the critical section given to process i.

Design Specifications/Requirements

To synthesize an efficient arbiter, we must express the precise design specifications. We can express these specifications formally in Linear Temporal Logic (LTL). We classify the design specifications as Safety and Functionality/Fairness.

Safety Specifications
  1. Mutual Exclusion Condition:

    We must ensure that no two processes can access the critical section at the same time. We can express in LTL as:

    G (!g1 & !g2)

Functionality Specifications
  1. Fairness:

    The arbitrer must ensure fairness, i.e., every request made by any process must be eventually granted. We can express in LTL as:

    G (r(i) -> F g(i))
