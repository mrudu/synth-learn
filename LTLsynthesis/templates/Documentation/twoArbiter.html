In this example, we consider three processes P and Q that perform operations on/interacts with a critical section. Our goal is to synthesize an efficient arbiter which will control access of these processes to the critical section.

Design Specifications/Requirements

To synthesize an efficient arbiter, we must express the precise design specifications. We can express these specifications formally in Linear Temporal Logic (LTL). The atomic propositions p, q represent the requests made by processes P and Q. The atomic propositions gp, gq represent the accesses granted to P and Q. We classify the design specifications as Safety and Functionality/Fairness.

Safety Specifications

We must ensure that no two processes can access the critical section at the same time. We can express in LTL as: G (!gp & !gq)

Functionality Specifications

The arbitrer must ensure fairness, i.e., every request made by any process must be eventually granted. We can express in LTL as: G (p -> F gp) & G (q -> F gq)
