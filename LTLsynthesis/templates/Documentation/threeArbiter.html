In this example, we consider three processes P, Q and R that perform operations on/interacts with a critical section. Our goal is to synthesize an efficient arbiter which will control access of these processes to the critical section.

Design Specifications/Requirements

To synthesize an efficient arbiter, we must express the precise design specifications. We can express these specifications formally in Linear Temporal Logic (LTL). The atomic propositions p, q, r represent the requests made by processes P, Q and R. The atomic propositions gp, gq, gr represent the accesses granted to P, Q and R. We classify the design specifications as Safety and Functionality.

Safety Specifications

We must ensure that no two processes can access the critical section at the same time. We can express in LTL as: G ((!gp & !gr) | (!gq & !gr) | (!gp & !gq))

Functionality Specifications

Every request made must be granted. We can express in LTL as: G (p -> F gp) & G (q -> F gq) & G (r -> F gr)
